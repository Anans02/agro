Bacterial Wilt is a serious disease in pulses (such as beans, peas, and lentils) caused by Ralstonia solanacearum. It primarily affects the vascular system of plants, leading to wilting and eventual death.

Symptoms

1.Wilting:

  • Sudden wilting, starting from lower leaves, progressing upwards.

2.Leaf Symptoms:

  • Dull green, curled leaves, with no significant yellowing before wilting.

3.Stem:

  • Brown discoloration in vascular tissue, with a milky bacterial ooze when cut.

4.Root Rot:

  • Rotten roots, often with a foul smell.

5.Plant Death:

  • Entire plant wilts and dies as the disease progresses.

Precautions

1.Resistant Varieties:

  • Plant resistant pulse varieties.

2.Crop Rotation:

  • Rotate with non-host crops like cereals.

3.Soil Health:

  • Ensure good drainage and avoid waterlogging.

Sanitation:

  • Remove infected plants and sterilize tools.

Treatments

1.Soil Solarization:

  • Use plastic sheets to heat the soil and kill bacteria.

2.Biological Control:

  • Apply biocontrol agents like Trichoderma or Pseudomonas fluorescens.

3.Chemical Control:

  • Copper-based bactericides may limit early spread.

Grafting:

  • Use resistant rootstocks for grafting.

Seed Selection

  • Use disease-free seeds from certified sources.
