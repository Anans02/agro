Seedling blight can be caused by various pathogens, including fungi, bacteria, and viruses.

Seedling blight in pulses is a significant issue for farmers growing legumes like chickpeas, lentils, and beans.

Symptoms

  • The seedlings may suddenly wilt and collapse.
  • Might show yellowing leaves, indicating poor nutrient uptake
  • Stems may show dark, sunken spots that can girdle the plant, causing it to wilt

Precautions

  • Purchase seeds from reputable suppliers to ensure they are disease-free
  • Treat seeds with appropriate fungicides or bactericides before planting
  • Make sure of timely planting of the crop

Treatments

  • Apply fungicides to seeds before planting like Thiram, Carboxin etc
  • Improve soil drainage to prevent waterlogging.
