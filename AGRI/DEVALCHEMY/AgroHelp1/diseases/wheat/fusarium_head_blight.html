Fusarium Head Blight (FHB), also known as Wheat Scab, is a serious disease affecting wheat and other cereal crops. It is caused by the fungus Fusarium graminearum.

Symptoms

1. Appearance

  • Heads: The primary symptom is the appearance of bleached or white heads that look “scabby.” Affected grains may turn light brown or pinkish and can be covered with a pink to reddish mold.
  • Kernels: Kernels become shriveled, light-colored, and may have visible mold growth.
  • General: Infected plants may show premature browning and dieback of the heads.

2. Location

  • Heads and Kernels: Primarily affects the heads or spikes of wheat and barley, but can also infect stems and leaves in severe cases.

3. Damage

  • Yield: Leads to reduced grain yield and quality.
  • Mycotoxins: The fungus produces mycotoxins which can make the grain unsuitable for human consumption and animal feed.

Precautions

1. Resistant Varieties

  • Varieties: Plant wheat and barley varieties that are resistant or less susceptible to Fusarium Head Blight.

2. Crop Rotation

  • Rotation: Avoid planting cereals in fields where Fusarium Head Blight has been a problem previously. Rotate with non-host crops to reduce pathogen buildup.

3. Field Management

  • Drainage: Ensure proper field drainage to reduce moisture levels, as Fusarium thrives in wet conditions.
  • Irrigation: Avoid overhead irrigation, which increases leaf wetness and creates favorable conditions for the disease.

Treatments

1. Fungicides

  • Application: Apply fungicides during the flowering period of wheat for effective control. Products containing active ingredients like tebuconazole or prothioconazole are often used. Follow local guidelines for application rates and timings.

2. Cultural Practices

  • Timing: Apply fungicides early in the infection cycle, usually at the beginning of flowering.
  • Field Management: Maintain proper plant spacing to improve airflow and reduce humidity around the crop.

3. Mycotoxin Management

  • Handling: If mycotoxins are detected in the grain, properly segregate and handle the infected grain to prevent contamination of the food supply.
