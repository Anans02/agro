Maize rusts are generally easy to identify by the appearance of brown pustules.

It rarely reaches levels that cause yield loss in commercial hybrids. It is most problematic during prolonged periods of cool, wet weather

Symptoms

  • The fungus causes brown spots on both leaf sides
  • Under severe conditions leaf chlorosis and death may occur
  • On resistant cultivars the fungus produces tiny dark specks.

Precautions

  • Monitor your crop regularly for signs of the diseases.
  • Avoid over watering of the crop
  • Plan a crop rotation with non-susceptibe crops

Treatments

  • Use resistant corn hybrids.
  • Fungicides can also be beneficial, especially if applied early when few pustules have appeared on the leaves.
