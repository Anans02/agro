Turcicum Leaf Blight is a fungal disease distributed widely throughout the world and causing significant yield losses

The disease is more prevalent in humid weather with temperature between 20-30 degree Celcius

Symptoms

  • The fungus causes small cigar-shaped lesions to complete destruction of the foliage
  • Under severe conditions leaf chlorosis and death may occur
  • On resistant cultivars the fungus produces tiny dark specks.

Precautions

  • Reducing maize residue
  • Applying foliar fungicides
  • Make sure of timely planting of the crop

Treatments

  • Minimize the amount of infected maize residue on the soil surface to reduce spore production and infection
  • Fungicides can also be beneficial such as mancozeb, carbendazim, or combinations of these, applied at the right stage of plant growth, to reduce disease severity.
