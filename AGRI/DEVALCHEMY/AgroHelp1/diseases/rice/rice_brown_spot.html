Brown spot is a fungal disease that can infect both seedlings and mature plants.

Its most observable damage is the numerous big spots on the leaves which can kill the whole leaf. When infection occurs in the seed, unfilled grains or spotted or discolored seeds are formed.

Symptoms

  • The fungus may cause black discoloration of the roots
  • Infected seedlings are stunted or killed
  • On resistant cultivars the fungus produces tiny dark specks.

Precautions

  • Provide well balanced nutrients for the soil.
  • Avoid water stress conditions
  • Clean all rice debris and weeds from fields which sources of inoculum.

Treatments

  • Fungicides: Use fungicides (e.g., iprodione, propiconazole, trifloxystrobin, and carbendazim) as seed treatments.
  • Treat seeds with hot water (53−54°C) for 10−12 minutes before planting, to control primary infection at the seedling stage. To increase effectiveness of treatment, pre-soak seeds in cold water for eight hours.
